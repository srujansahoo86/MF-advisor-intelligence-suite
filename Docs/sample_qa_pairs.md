## Q3 — What is TER and How Does It Affect Returns?

**Source type:** M2 — Fee Explainer

**Question:** What does TER mean and how does it affect my returns?

**Answer:** TER stands for **Total Expense Ratio** — the annual fee a mutual fund charges to cover its operating costs (fund management, administration, marketing, etc.). It is expressed as a percentage of the fund's average daily net assets and is deducted daily from the NAV before it is published. A fund with a TER of 0.5% effectively reduces your gross returns by 0.5% per year. Lower TER means more of the market return stays with the investor.

**Citation:** [SEBI Circular on Expense Ratios](https://www.sebi.gov.in/sebi_data/attachdocs/1475063737177.pdf)

## Q4 — Why Is the Expense Ratio Lower in a Direct Plan?

**Source type:** M2 — Fee Explainer

**Question:** Why is the expense ratio lower in a direct plan vs regular plan?

**Answer:** In a **regular plan**, the fund house pays a trail commission to the distributor (mutual fund advisor or broker) who sold you the fund. This commission is bundled into the expense ratio, making it higher. In a **direct plan**, you invest without going through a distributor, so no trail commission is paid. The savings are passed on to you as a lower TER — typically 0.5%–1.0% lower per year — which compounds significantly over a long investment horizon.

**Citation:** [AMFI Investor Corner](https://www.amfiindia.com/investor-corner)

## Q5 — Exit Load and Lock-in Period for ELSS Funds on Kuvera

**Source type:** M1 + M2 crossover

**Question:** What is the exit load and lock-in period for an ELSS fund on Kuvera?

**Answer:** ELSS (Equity Linked Savings Scheme) funds on Kuvera have a **mandatory 3-year lock-in period** from the date of each SIP instalment or lump-sum purchase. Units cannot be redeemed before the lock-in expires. After 3 years, **no exit load is charged** — you can redeem at any time without penalty. The lock-in also qualifies for a tax deduction of up to ₹1.5 lakh per year under Section 80C of the Income Tax Act.

**Citation:** [Kuvera ELSS Help](https://support.kuvera.in/article/105-what-is-elss)
